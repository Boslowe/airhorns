Executive Overview

Prepared for: Paul S. Rafelson, Esq., Rafelson Law PLLC
Date: May 1, 2026

The matter

27 competitor air horn ASINs on Amazon were physically test bought. Forensic review of the physical product photos, packaging, listing copy, and seller storefronts identified federal regulatory violations across all 27 ASINs. Every ASIN has at least 3 distinct federal violations. Most have 4 to 6.

The DOT SP 11516 substance scope is dispositive

The DOT-SP 11516 base permit text (16th Revision, August 3, 2018), issued by PHMSA, is on this site at SP11516_base_permit_text_16th_revision.pdf. Paragraph 6 of that document lists the closed set of hazardous materials authorized under the permit. R-134a (1,1,1,2-Tetrafluoroethane) as a pure substance is not on the list. Tetrafluoroethane appears only as a 30%-70% mixture component with dimethyl ether under UN1954.

This means every can in this matter that prints "DOT SP 11516" while declaring R-134a as its contents is misrepresenting compliance with a permit that does not authorize that substance, in violation of 49 CFR 171.2(g) and 49 CFR 171.2(j). The violation is established on the face of the can and is independent of who supplied the cans, of the supply chain, and of the AIM Act argument.

Family takedown coverage

Most of the 27 test buy ASINs are not standalone listings. They are individual variations within larger product family listings that also include 2 pack, 4 pack, 6 pack, and color variants under the same parent. Each per ASIN brief lists the additional child ASIN variations in parentheses under the test buy ASIN, so they are covered when the test buy listing is removed.

Seller cluster pattern

The top 8 sellers control 22 of the 27 ASINs. Several sellers operate multiple ASINs as color variants within a single family listing. Full seller cluster table is in the master Excel on the "Seller Clusters" sheet.

Strongest legal arguments in decreasing order

The 27 ASINs are subject to multiple overlapping federal violations. The arguments below are ordered by evidentiary strength, lowest to highest evidentiary burden. Most ASINs are caught by several of these arguments simultaneously.

1. False marking under 49 CFR 171.2(g): SP 11516 does not authorize R-134a

Lowest evidentiary burden. The SP 11516 base permit text (paragraph 6) lists the closed set of substances authorized under the permit, and R-134a is not on the list. Any can printing "DOT SP 11516" while declaring R-134a as its contents is making a false representation of compliance under 49 CFR 171.2(g) and 171.2(j) regardless of the supply chain.

ASINs caught: B0CLYJJ7T7, B0CNXTKMYP, B0D152922F, B0D5LL9JSV, B0D7VY1ZHL, B0DJVC96J8, B0DJW1HJRF, B0DK6KFSZM, B0FRMYBZBG (9 ASINs)

2. EPA AIM Act, R 134a banned in aerosols since January 1, 2025, with manufacturing date evidence

For ASINs with manufacturing dates printed on the product itself that postdate January 1, 2025, the pre ban inventory defense is impossible. The product is its own evidence.

ASINs caught:

  • B0D152922F (MFG date 10/31/2025)
  • B0D6VYJ95D (MFG date 09-07-2025)
  • B0D7VY1ZHL (MFG date 08/13/2025)
  • B0DJW1HJRF (MFG date 08/13/2025)
  • B0DK6KFSZM (MFG date 12/29/2025)
  • B0FDKB1J36 (MFG date 26-08-2025)

3. EPA AIM Act, R 134a explicitly stated on the can label

For ASINs that explicitly state "1,1,1,2 Tetrafluoroethane" or "R 134a" on the can label, the AIM Act prohibition applies regardless of manufacturing date in any practical sense, given that these listings are actively restocked and sold in 2026.

ASINs caught: B0CLYJJ7T7, B0CNXTKMYP, B0CZNZV8TZ, B0D152922F, B0D5HB8CSL, B0D7B4KF7Q, B0D7VY1ZHL, B0D97CRVM2, B0DJVC96J8, B0DJW1HJRF, B0DK6KFSZM, B0F2154Z5M, B0F5HR7FQ9, B0F62YLXK6, B0FRMYBZBG, B0G648CVCJ (16 ASINs)

4. Hazmat marking violation, refrigerant identity omitted from packaging

49 CFR 172.301(a) and 172.101 require pressurized aerosol packages to be marked with the proper shipping name and UN identification number (UN1950 for flammable aerosols). For ASINs that omit chemical identity entirely, the failure to mark the proper shipping name is itself a federal hazmat violation, independent of the AIM Act.

ASINs caught: B0D5LL9JSV, B0D6VYJ95D, B0DGLCM141, B0DT91W8NL, B0DT91YK9Q, B0F1DC3JRM, B0F1DH5WLX, B0F38GB4W9, B0FCXCDPQ1, B0FDKB1J36, B0FFGQWXHG (11 ASINs)

5. No DOT permit marking on a regulated hazmat aerosol

For ASINs that show no DOT special permit number at all on a pressurized container, this is an additional hazmat shipping violation under 49 CFR Part 173.

ASINs caught: B0CZNZV8TZ, B0D5HB8CSL, B0D6VYJ95D, B0D7B4KF7Q, B0DGLCM141, B0DT91YK9Q, B0F1DC3JRM, B0F1DH5WLX, B0F2154Z5M, B0F38GB4W9, B0F5HR7FQ9, B0FCXCDPQ1, B0FDKB1J36, B0G648CVCJ (14 ASINs)

6. FIFRA, unregistered pesticidal claims

For ASINs that make pesticidal claims (bear deterrent, dog deterrent, scare wildlife, etc.) without an EPA Establishment Number, this triggers FIFRA jurisdiction under 7 U.S.C. 136 et seq. The Amazon listings themselves carry the violation, regardless of can contents.

ASINs caught: B0CLYJJ7T7, B0CNXTKMYP, B0CZNZV8TZ, B0D152922F, B0D5HB8CSL, B0D5LL9JSV, B0D6VYJ95D, B0D7B4KF7Q, B0D7VY1ZHL, B0D97CRVM2, B0DGLCM141, B0DJVC96J8, B0DJW1HJRF, B0DK6KFSZM, B0DT91W8NL, B0DT91YK9Q, B0F1DC3JRM, B0F1DH5WLX, B0F2154Z5M, B0F38GB4W9, B0F5HR7FQ9, B0F62YLXK6, B0FCXCDPQ1, B0FDKB1J36, B0G648CVCJ (25 ASINs)

Most ASINs are caught by 3 to 5 of these arguments simultaneously. Per ASIN briefs document the full violation set for each individual listing.
